# REG-AE-CBUAE-AML · UAE Central Bank — AML/CFT Guidelines

**Document ID:** REG-AE-CBUAE-AML-v1
**Jurisdiction:** AE
**Regulator:** CBUAE (Central Bank of the UAE) — jointly with SCA and DFSA/FSRA for free-zone entities
**Primary Source:** CBUAE AML/CFT Guidelines for Licensed Financial Institutions (2021, amended 2023)
**Related Legislation:** Federal Decree-Law No. 20 of 2018 on AML; Cabinet Decision No. 10 of 2019
**Source Type:** external_regulatory
**Rule Nexus:** R174, R181

---

## 1. Reporting to goAML

Licensed financial institutions must report to the **UAE Financial Intelligence Unit (UAE FIU)** via the **goAML** portal. Reporting types include:
- **STR / SAR** — filed upon suspicion, no monetary threshold.
- **FFR (Funds Freeze Report)** — for sanctions matches.
- **RFI (Request for Information)** responses to FIU queries.

## 2. Cross-Border and Correspondent Banking

The UAE sits on major East–West payment corridors. CBUAE Guidelines §6 impose enhanced measures on:
- Correspondent banking with respondents in higher-risk jurisdictions.
- Wire transfers where originator or beneficiary information is incomplete.
- Trade-based money laundering patterns via free-zone entities.

**R174 nexus:** the cross-border layering rule for AE deployments consults FATF-status and wire-info-completeness signals held in the RTCA.

## 3. Cash Handling

While the UAE has no fixed CTR threshold analogous to the US BSA, currency declaration at borders is required for amounts **≥ AED 60,000 or equivalent** under Cabinet Decision 10/2019. Enhanced scrutiny is expected for repeated cash activity within short windows.

## 4. Sanctions Compliance

Institutions must screen against:
- **UN Consolidated Sanctions List**.
- **UAE Local Terrorist List**.
- **OFAC / EU / UK sanctions** where risk-relevant.

Sanctions hits are handled outside R174/R181 — this is a separate deterministic screening layer.

## 5. Record Retention

**Five years** minimum from the date of the transaction or termination of the business relationship, per Federal Decree-Law 20/2018.
